Company overview

About Workshop Coffee

Premium speciality coffee built around quality, transparency and craftsmanship.

Workshop Coffee is a UK speciality coffee roaster that sources coffee directly from producers and sells premium coffee beans, subscriptions, brewing equipment and café experiences. The company differentiates itself through ethical sourcing, transparency and high-quality roasting, targeting coffee enthusiasts, home baristas and consumers who value craftsmanship (Workshop Coffee, n.d.).

Unlike mass-market brands, Workshop Coffee competes with speciality coffee companies such as Square Mile Coffee, Origin Coffee and Pact Coffee. As a result, its social media strategy should prioritise education, authenticity and community building, as speciality coffee consumers are more likely to value expertise and product quality than price-led promotions.

The following social media marketing plan aims to increase brand awareness, customer engagement and online sales while reinforcing Workshop Coffee's premium market position.

Task 1

Social Media Marketing Plan

Three SMART objectives supported by targeted activities, justified recommendations and measurable performance indicators.

01

Objective 1

Increase brand awareness

SMART objective: Increase Instagram followers by 20% within six months.

Workshop Coffee should increase its use of short-form video content on Instagram Reels and TikTok, focusing on brewing techniques, barista demonstrations, coffee origins and behind-the-scenes footage from its roasting process. Even though the company is already producing high-quality photos, it will be primarily the video content that social media algorithms will promote and that will help to promote the company (Manic, 2024). For a specialty coffee company, educational content that allows customers to understand the quality of products is much more valuable than just promotional posts.

The company should look for cooperation with the well-known experts and home baristas, but not with the influencers. Micro-influencers generally have smaller but more engaged audiences, making their recommendations appear more authentic and credible (Joshi et al., 2025). Companies like Square Mile Coffee and Pact Coffee are already engaging in the use of educational videos to present their expertise in brewing and their knowledge about coffee. Adopting a similar strategy would help Workshop Coffee reach new audiences while reinforcing its premium positioning rather than competing on price.

Instagram Reels TikTok Brewing tutorials Behind the scenes Micro-influencers
02

Objective 2

Increase customer engagement

SMART objective: Increase the social media engagement rate from 4% to 6% within six months.

Workshop Coffee should promote two-way communication by using Instagram Stories to run polls, quizzes, coffee brewing Q&A sessions and get customer feedback. Unlike merely product-promoting, these functionalities encourage customers to be involved in conversations, thereby enriching the emotional tie with the brand. Gu and Duan (2024) assume that interactive content increases consumer engagement as it makes users active participants instead of passive viewers.

Educational content should also take bigger place in the content strategy. Brewing tutorials, coffee tasting notes and insights into different coffee origins will boost Workshop Coffee’s credibility and create real value for the customers. This strategy will facilitate company’s positioning as premium as consumers of specialty coffee usually search for knowledge prior to making any purchases. Higher engagement is also good news for social media algorithms as it indicates useful content, decreasing the need for pricey advertising in future.

Polls Quizzes Q&A sessions Tasting notes Customer feedback
03

Objective 3

Increase online sales

SMART objective: Increase website sales generated through social media by 15% within 12 months.

Social media content should focus on emphasizing coffee subscription, seasonal items and coffee brewing appliances through short-form videos and customer demonstrations. Subscription services should receive particular attention because they generate recurring revenue and strengthen customer loyalty compared with one-off purchases.

Retargeting campaigns should also be used to lure customers back who visited and interacted with Workshop Coffee's social media account even without completing the purchase. These customers have shown their intention to buy a product and therefore have higher chances of making a purchase than completely new customers. According to Pan et al. (2025), influencer marketing and social commerce features increase the likelihood of making a purchase online. If Workshop Coffee combines the educational content with retargeting, it is going to increase the conversion rate as well as customer lifetime value, rather than just increase the traffic in the website.

Subscriptions Seasonal products Product demonstrations Retargeting Social commerce

Task 2

Improving Customer Conversion Rates

Five practical activities designed to remove purchase barriers, build trust, improve retention and increase revenue.

Workshop Coffee is engaged in a competitive specialty coffee industry where customers are willing to pay premium prices for quality, reliability, and smooth online purchasing. Since the business is heavily reliant on online sales and subscription service, increasing conversion rates must focus on both attracting new customers and eliminating barriers to purchase and retaining them. The following actions aim to drive the revenue growth while establishing long-term customer loyalty and boosting conversions.

01

Optimise the website and checkout process

Workshop Coffee has to make sure its website keeps improving by making navigation easier, increasing speed of its pages and introducing quicker methods of paying, like Apple Pay or Google Pay. Since those buying premium coffee products tend to compare them with other products, complex checkout may lead to lost sales. Kingsnorth (2022) states that reducing customer friction in their journey to make a purchase you increase conversion rates.

Higher rates of conversion and lower rates of cart abandonment imply higher number of visitors completing purchases thus increasing revenue and leaving advertising expenses unchanged.

Focus:Speed · Navigation · Checkout · Payment options
02

Personalise email marketing and retargeting campaigns

Customers who explore products or abandon the shopping cart should receive suitable emails or remarketing ads about previously viewed products, coffee subscriptions, or seasonal deals. Personalisation is more relevant to the client, motivating him to come back and buy (Kotler et al., 2017). This is especially important for Workshop Coffee since coffee subscriptions provide a steady source of income, meaning that even a slight increase in conversion rates can increase the lifetime value of the customer.

Focus:Abandoned carts · Relevant offers · Subscriptions · CLV
03

Increase user-generated content and customer reviews

Workshop Coffee should stimulate customers to post photos, videos, and reviews about brewing under the company's hashtags and offer monthly prizes. Speciality coffee consumers tend to refer to their peers’ advice regarding a new coffee-maker and therefore trust more to real customers’ content as opposed to advertisements. Such companies as Origin Coffee and Pact Coffee actively cooperate with their customers to promote their brewing on social networks, thereby using user-generated content to ensure their credibility in the speciality coffee sphere. Increased confidence means customers are more likely to start buying the company’s products and thus boost its credibility.

Focus:Reviews · Hashtags · Monthly prizes · Social proof
04

Work with niche coffee influencers

Instead of collaborating with big influencers in the lifestyle space, Workshop Coffee should connect with reliable coffee producers who already have a following of customers interested in specialty coffee. The approach resonates more with what the company is trying to achieve. Even though the number of impressions may decrease, the quality of generated leads means fewer wasted marketing dollars due to intent to buy which already exists among target customers.

Focus:Credibility · Qualified traffic · Purchase intent · ROI
05

Promote subscription services through social media

Workshop Coffee should focus more on promoting coffee subscriptions than just single goods. Compared to one-time purchasers, subscribers generate consistent revenue and are more likely to become repeat consumers. Since it is more economical to keep existing customers than to find new ones, increasing subscription sales would eventually result in a more reliable revenue stream while lowering client acquisition expenses. The overall focus of these activities is on removing purchase barriers, building customer confidence, and boosting repeat purchases. They are intended to enhance the quality of future customer interactions.

Focus:Recurring revenue · Retention · Repeat purchases · Lower acquisition costs

Task 3

Recommendation for an Innovative Digital Marketing Process

A future-facing concept designed to replace interruption with timely, consent-based customer support.

The rise of social media has opened a new communication channel for businesses, and yet, with the emergence of several competing brands, users are swamped with advertisements and marketing messages. This means that digital marketing is possibly entering a new phase that lies within the predictive domain rather than in the advertising sphere.

The concept I propose is Predictive Lifestyle Marketing (PLM). Rather than using AI and contextual advertising like today's marketing models that rely on users' browsing history and behaviour, PLM will use the data collected from connected devices such as smart coffee machines and wearable technology with the customers' consent (Atzori, Iera and Morabito, 2010). Rather than bombarding customers with advertisements, the system will identify when customers need a product and advise on it accordingly.

As for Workshop Coffee, this might mean a smart coffee machine which detects the low amount of coffee beans and suggests to subscribe for a refill through Workshop Coffee app. On a chilly weekend, the system can recommend buying dark roast or a special seasonal blend based on last purchases and weather conditions. This would create an impression of recommendations not as an advertisement, but as a service that facilitates customer experience.

In contrast to existing digital marketing, product location marketing would not base on social media algorithms, cookies or users’ search history. The PLM would not interrupt consumers with advertisements, as the product recommendation will appear only when there is an actual need of a person in a specific product. This kind of approach can enhance customer experience.

Even though this method is not currently used as a complete marketing tool, the developments in AI, IoT and smart home technologies may make it work in the future (Atzori, Iera and Morabito, 2010).
